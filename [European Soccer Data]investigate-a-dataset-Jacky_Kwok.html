    countries = pd.read_sql_query("SELECT * from Country", con)
    matches = pd.read_sql_query("SELECT * from Match", con)

    teams = pd.read_sql_query("SELECT * from Team", con)
    player = pd.read_sql_query("SELECT * from Player",con)
    player_attributes = pd.read_sql_query("SELECT * from Player_Attributes",con)
    sequence = pd.read_sql_query("SELECT * from sqlite_sequence",con)
    team_attributes = pd.read_sql_query("SELECT * from Team_Attributes",con)

In [35]:
def tag(df):
    if df.home_team_goal > df.away_team_goal :
        return "Home win"
    elif df.home_team_goal < df.away_team_goal :
        return "Away win"
    elif df.home_team_goal == df.away_team_goal :
        return "Draw"

Conclusions¶

Qestions and findings¶

1. Is match getting more boring now(in terms of the goals per match)?¶

No,during season 08/09 to season 15/16. there is a trend of the goals per match increasing but it is not significant. The difference of between Max and Min less than 7%.¶

2. Is there any home team adventage?¶

Yes, we found that overall home team win rate is about 46% while away team only 29%. We also look at the goals by different countries. It is obvious that home team has more goals than away team in all countries.¶

3. Which factors contribute to the overall-rating of a player?¶

By using Bar plot and Heatmap, it shows that the top 10 factors are 'potential', 'reactions', 'vision', 'long_passing','penalties', 'short_passing', 'shot_power', 'ball_control','long_shots', 'positioning'.¶

Limitations¶

1. There are very small portion missing data in the dataframe. We have to ignore these set of data.¶

2. For study the goal trend, we only have 8 years data, which is not long enough to get a signaificant trend.¶

3. For study the overall-rating of a player, we droped the attacking_work_rate and defensive_work_rate columns, since they are not quantitative data.¶
